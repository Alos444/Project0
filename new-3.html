  • Istanbul is the only city in the world that connects two continents Europe and Asia.
  • Istanbul was the capital of 4 empires.
  • The Grand Bazaar is open in 1461 and is one of the oldest covered markets in the world and has about 60 streets and 5000 shops.
  • The third oldest tube line after London and New York was open in 1875 in Istanbul.
  • Istanbul was built in the image of Rome - on seven hills.
  • The only city with the greatest temples of two world religions,temple buildings of Islam( Blue Mosque) and Orthodoxy(Hagia Sophya).
  • During the history of Constantinople, 10 emperors of the Roman Empire, 82 Byzantine rulers and 30 Sultans of the Ottoman Empire lived and ruled by their powers in it.
  • The luxurious Chiragan Hotel in Istanbul prepares one of the most expensive desserts in the world - a golden cake. Fruits for filling are kept in a special composition for several years, and the finished cake is covered with 24-carat gold on top.
  • The Istanbul Church of St. Stephen is the only entirely iron church on Earth that was built from this unusual material to avoid arson.
  • Two of the seven ancient wonders of the world were in Turkey: the Temple of Artemis at Ephesus and the Mausoleum in Halicarnassus.
  • St. Nicholas, also known as Santa Claus, was born in the city of Patara (Patara). He served as a bishop in a church in Demre, on the Mediterranean coast of Turkey. The church exists to this day.
  • Kissing the hand of an elderly person is a traditional sign of respect in Turkey.
